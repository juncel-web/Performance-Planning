Performance Standard Rating Scale — Reference

Keep this in view throughout the workshop. Rating 3 is your primary target — the minimum acceptable standard.

RatingLevelDescription
1UnsatisfactoryFar below the defined standard. Immediate corrective action required.
2DevelopingBelow standard. An improvement plan is in place or needed.
3Meets StandardConsistently meets the minimum acceptable standard. This is your target.
4Exceeds StandardAbove standard. Strong performance demonstrating departmental capability.
5OutstandingSignificantly exceeds all standards. Best-in-class or benchmark performance.

Opening & Context

30 minutes

Activity Instructions
  • In the space below, write one sentence that describes what success looks like in your supply chain role.
  • Share your sentence with the group and listen to others' responses.
💬

Reflection Prompt: In one sentence, what does success look like in your supply chain role?

Key Points — Why KPIs Matter
  • → KPIs connect daily actions to company-level goals: cash flow, efficiency, client satisfaction.
  • → KPIs remove guesswork — they give clarity and accountability.
  • → The most powerful KPIs are ones you designed yourself.
  • → KPIs are living tools — they evolve as your department grows.
Block 2

Strategic Alignment

45 minutes
"Strategic objectives are the company's destination. Department objectives are the route your team takes. Functional objectives are the steps each person takes along that route. Every KPI you build must trace back to this hierarchy."
Activity Instructions
  • Write one Strategic Objective your department supports (company-level goal).
  • Write one Department Objective that connects to it.
  • Write one Functional Objective — your team's day-to-day priority that supports the department goal.
  • Share your department objective with your group.

Block 4

Converting Indicators into KPIs

60 minutes
"A KPI is a number that tells us if the success indicator is happening. 'Inventory accuracy' becomes '% variance between system and physical count.' KPIs give us clarity — they remove guesswork and create accountability."
Activity Instructions
  • For each of your success indicators, complete a KPI template below.
  • Write a clear Definition (what exactly does it measure?) and Formula (how do you calculate it?).
  • Define what each performance rating (1–5) looks like specifically for your KPI.
  • Discuss: "What evidence output will prove this KPI is being met?"
Supply Chain KPI Examples — Reference

Inventory% Inventory Accuracy(Accurate Items ÷ Total Items Counted) × 100~85%≥95%
